5 GAMES TO PLAY WITH YOUR DOG

Let's get some fun toys to play with your lovely dog!

1. Tug of War

Another classic game most dogs love is playing tug of war. Great for both indoor or outdoor play, tug of war is fun and engaging for your dog. Plus, all that pulling and tugging is a good source of physical exercise. Avoid overexciting your dog with this game, and don't let children play this one.

A few things to keep in mind: be careful if you're playing tug of war with a puppy. Remember, puppies' mouths have not fully formed, and they still have their baby teeth. Only pull gently for a few seconds at a time. If they seem too little, explore other play activities.

Keep your dog's tooth and gum health in mind when playing tug of war. Use something that won't hurt the insides of their mouth. Try using a rope, or a dog toy made from soft rubber. If your dog has a history of back and neck injuries, try another activity until talking with your veterinarian.

2. Fetch

Almost every dog gets excited over a game of fetch, as it's a classic game for humans and dogs alike. All you need is a yard, park or field, a tennis ball or frisbee and an enthusiastic dog to have some fun.

Fetch is an excellent game for energetic dogs. It provides plenty of physical exercise while helping them fulfill their instinct to run. It can also keep your dog mentally engaged and challenged by trying different objects for him to run after. For example, a frisbee may be a bit trickier to catch yet more fun to snag in midair.Try adjusting the height, distance and direction of your throws to keep your dog interested. Don't forget to praise your dog each time they return their toy to you so you can toss it one more time.

3. Hide the Treat or Hide & Seek

If you're looking to play mentally stimulating mind games for dogs, you can't go wrong with hide the treat. This dog enrichment game is a fun way for them to exercise their problem-solving skills. Simply leave a trail by rubbing the treat on a few spots along the way to its hiding space, then place it somewhere that's challenging for your dog to find.

As your dog searches, try using language like "hot" and "cold" as your dog gets closer and further away from the treat. Changing your tone from a calm "cold" to an excited "warm" or "hot" will build their listening skills, though it will also be a hoot to see your dog's excitement as they get closer to the treat. If you're feeling extra adventurous, try playing a round of hide and seek with your dog. It's a great way to keep them on their toes while spending some quality time together.

4. Obstacle Course

An obstacle course is a great way to provide physical and mental exercise for your dog. You can create one using household items like pillows, chairs, and cardboard boxes, or use items specifically designed for dog agility courses. Set up a series of obstacles and have your dog navigate through them, encouraging them with treats and praise as they go.

Not only is an obstacle course a fun activity for your dog, but it can also help improve their coordination, balance, and focus. Plus, it's a great way to bond with your dog and keep them entertained for hours on end.

5. Training & Tricks

Training and teaching your dog tricks not only helps to keep their minds sharp, but it's also a great bonding activity. Plus, it's a fun way to show off your dog's impressive skills to friends and family. Start with basic commands like "sit," "stay," and "come," and then move on to more advanced tricks like "roll over" and "shake."

Training sessions should be kept short, around 15-20 minutes at a time, and should be filled with plenty of praise and treats for a job well done. As your dog becomes more skilled at tricks, be sure to vary the training sessions to keep things interesting for them.
